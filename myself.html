I was born in Towson, Maryland, US in 2009, on Canada Day (July 1st). We moved out of the US about 2 years after I was born, and because I only lived in the US for only ~1/6 of my life, the American culture hasn't really affected me that much. My family lived in the USA for only around 1 - 1.5 years before I was born, so my entire family isn't really considered American. I still acknowledge that my birthday is on Canada Day, and I am born in the US in Towson, but since it isn't really major, I just think of it for fun. For example, no one in Canada or the US would say "Oh look, that's an American!"

After we moved from the US, we travelled all the way to Saskatoon, Saskatchewan, where I currently live. So if you count my age, I have lived here for ~11 years. Canada has been a pretty big influence on me, but not that much on my parents. Both my parents are from China, and grew up there, but I have lived here for almost all my life, so it has changed me a lot. Many everyday things come from an Asian perspective, and the others come from a western view. Examples from Canada include: hockey/skating, weather, english, and some foods (such as pancakes).

China definitely has a big impact on my perception of the world. Both of my parents and sister were born in China. At home, it was always me, two parents, and my sister; all around me was chinese culture. As I grew up, I realized that the reason why the stereotype of "All Chinese people are "smart", that people believe, isn't because we are born "smart"; but that our parents push us harder and to do more. We celebrate traditional holidays, such as Chinese New Year, cook/bake chinese foods and play chinese sports/games (such as table tennis or chinese poker).

Overall, my family and siblings have affected me a lot. It's a big thing because I was born in the US, live in Canada right now, but my culture has pretty much all come from China. When I think of myself, I even have troubles understanding where i'm from. Even though I am from all places across the board, China definitely has the biggest influence on me right now, since it's all around me when i'm with my family. I always say that i'm a dual citizen (US & CAN), but it doesn't really feel like I really am a Canadian, or an American. I wasn't born in China, but since my family is from China, it feels like it dominates. In conclusion, I am a boy who is from around the world, but China has the biggest influence.
